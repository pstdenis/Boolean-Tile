The system represents a three-layer tower connecting Boolean logic through continuous truth values to quantum gates, unified by a single geometric object: the Bloch sphere.
Layer 1 — Boolean vertices: The 16 truth tables as 2×2 tiles. Each is a function f: {0,1}² → {0,1}. The 4 cells are the 4 corners of the unit square.
Layer 2 — Continuous extension (McNaughton functions): Evaluating each connective at bit-depth d gives a dyadic rational z = k/(2^d - 1) for every (p, q) ∈ [0,1]². As d → ∞, this converges to a unique piecewise-linear function — the McNaughton extension of the Boolean connective. The key structural fact: OR becomes min(1, p+q) because OR is carry-free addition, and every connective is bounded by Łukasiewicz operations.
Layer 3 — Lifting to the sphere: The coordinate map θ = π·p, φ = 2π·q sends the unit square to the Bloch sphere. The function value z = f(p,q) becomes radial displacement r = 1 + z·0.4. The 4 corners of the square become the 2 poles (p=0 north, p=1 south) crossed with the 2 meridians (q=0, q=1). The 4 IFS maps are exactly the 4 corner-contractions — each takes you "halfway toward a corner" — and the chaos game fills the attractor: the set of (θ, φ, z) satisfying the function's bounds.
Layer 4 — Quantum gates (§7): Each connective's truth table, written as a diagonal matrix diag(f(00), f(01), f(10), f(11)) with entries ±1 under Convention A (True=-1), conjugates by H⊗² to produce a 2-qubit unitary. XOR → X⊗X (a Bell-state generator), AND → entangling, the 8 monomials → pure tensor products, the 8 remaining → entangling superpositions.
Layer 5 — Circuit composition: Compound sentences become n-variable Boolean functions, whose Z-basis diagonal is the ±1 phase encoding on 2ⁿ basis states, and whose X-basis (H⊗ⁿ conjugated) yields the entanglement structure. The reduced density matrix of each qubit gives a Bloch vector plotted in the Circuit mode.
The whole structure can be seen as: 16 Boolean functions → 16 McNaughton surfaces → 16 unitaries on 2 qubits → 2ⁿ-parameter family of compound circuit states, all unified by the coordinate duality between the unit square and the Bloch sphere.
