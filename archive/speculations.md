## §15.4 — The Fibonacci Class and φ-Based Shepard Ratios

Among Kitaev's 16 phases, the ν = ±8 class is distinguished: it hosts the
Fibonacci anyon theory, where the non-Abelian anyon has quantum dimension
φ = (1+√5)/2 and braiding realizes the Fibonacci group.

In BTB, the 8 odd-DC (entangling) tiles use Shepard frequency ratios based on φ:
χ_∅ → 1, χ_A → φ², χ_B → φ, χ_AB → φ³.

Within the 8 entangling tiles, a further subdivision mirrors Kitaev's structure:
Pure entangling (|DC|=1, purity=1): AND, A∧¬B, ¬A∧B, NOR — 3 of 4 Walsh channels.
Mixed entangling (|DC|=3, purity=1/3): OR, B→A, A→B, NAND — all 4 channels.

The correspondence between φ and the BTB Shepard ratios: the three non-DC
frequency ratios {φ, φ², φ³} are exactly the ratios that appear in the Fibonacci
anyon fusion algebra F ⊗ F ≅ 1 ⊕ F, where the fusion matrix eigenvalues are
powers of φ. This suggests the Shepard projection may be a direct spectral
realization of the Fibonacci fusion algebra.
